RoboCup Rescue

Developing robots for disaster response and emergency situations

About RoboCup Rescue

RoboCup Rescue focuses on developing robotic systems for search and rescue operations in disaster scenarios. The league aims to promote research and development of robotic systems that can assist in real-world emergency situations, from urban search and rescue to disaster response coordination.

Robot League

Physical robots navigate through simulated disaster environments to locate and assist victims.

  • Autonomous navigation
  • Victim detection
  • Mapping and exploration
  • Real-time decision making

Simulation League

Virtual agents coordinate rescue operations in large-scale disaster simulations.

  • Multi-agent coordination
  • Resource allocation
  • Strategic planning
  • Communication protocols

Infrastructure League

Focus on communication systems and infrastructure for disaster response coordination.

  • Network resilience
  • Data management
  • Information systems
  • Coordination platforms

Technical Challenges

SLAM & Navigation

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping in unknown, dynamic environments with obstacles and debris.

Victim Detection

Identifying and locating victims using various sensors including thermal imaging and audio detection.

Communication

Maintaining reliable communication in challenging environments with limited infrastructure.

Multi-Robot Coordination

Coordinating multiple robots to work together efficiently in rescue operations.

UAE Participation

The UAE has shown growing interest in RoboCup Rescue, with several universities and research institutions developing rescue robotics capabilities. Given the UAE's focus on smart cities and emergency preparedness, this league aligns well with national priorities.

Active UAE Teams

  • UAEU Rescue Rangers - United Arab Emirates University
  • Khalifa Emergency Response - Khalifa University
  • AUS Disaster Tech - American University of Sharjah
  • Dubai Emergency Robotics - Dubai Municipality

Real-World Applications

Emergency Response

Robots can be deployed in dangerous situations where human responders cannot safely operate, such as building collapses or chemical spills.

Search Operations

Autonomous systems can cover large areas quickly and systematically search for missing persons in disaster zones.

Hazard Assessment

Robots equipped with specialized sensors can assess environmental hazards and provide critical information to rescue teams.

Supply Delivery

Autonomous vehicles can deliver medical supplies and emergency equipment to areas that are difficult to reach.
