RoboCup@Home

Service robots for everyday domestic environments and human-robot interaction

About RoboCup@Home

RoboCup@Home is the largest international annual competition for autonomous service robots. It focuses on the development of service and assistive robot technology with high relevance for future personal domestic applications.

Competition Categories

Domestic Standard Platform League (DSPL)

Toyota Human Support Robot (HSR) platform focusing on software development.

  • Standardized hardware platform
  • Focus on AI and software
  • Manipulation capabilities
  • Human-robot interaction

Open Platform League (OPL)

Custom robot platforms with diverse hardware solutions and approaches.

  • Custom robot designs
  • Hardware innovation
  • Diverse sensor systems
  • Flexible manipulation

Education League

Educational focus with simplified tasks for learning and development.

  • Educational objectives
  • Simplified scenarios
  • Learning-focused tasks
  • Student participation

Simulation League

Virtual environments for testing and developing service robot algorithms.

  • Gazebo simulation
  • ROS integration
  • Virtual environments
  • Algorithm testing

Competition Tasks

Speech & Person Recognition

Understanding natural language commands and recognizing people in domestic environments.

Object Manipulation

Grasping, carrying, and placing objects in realistic household scenarios.

Navigation & Mapping

Autonomous navigation in cluttered, dynamic home environments.

Human-Robot Interaction

Natural interaction with humans including gesture recognition and social behavior.

Shopping & Errands

Assisting with daily tasks like shopping, cleaning, and household management.

Restaurant Service

Serving customers in restaurant-like environments with complex interactions.

Technical Challenges

Natural and intuitive communication with humans

Understanding and mapping domestic environments semantically
