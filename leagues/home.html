RoboCup@Home

Service robots for everyday domestic environments

About RoboCup@Home

RoboCup@Home is the largest international annual competition for autonomous service robots. It aims to develop service and assistive robot technology with high relevance for future personal domestic applications. The competition focuses on real-world applications and human-robot interaction in dynamic environments.

Competition Stages

1

Robot Inspection

Teams demonstrate their robot's basic capabilities including navigation, manipulation, and human-robot interaction.

  • Safety requirements
  • Basic functionality
  • Technical interview
  • Poster presentation
2

Stage I Tests

Standardized tests focusing on specific abilities like speech recognition, object manipulation, and person following.

  • Speech & Person Recognition
  • Navigation & Mapping
  • Manipulation & Grasping
  • Human-Robot Interaction
3

Stage II Tests

More complex tasks that combine multiple abilities in realistic scenarios with increased difficulty.

  • Serving Drinks
  • Storing Groceries
  • Clean Up
  • Receptionist
4

Finals

Open demonstration where teams showcase their robot's capabilities in a realistic home environment.

  • Open Challenge
  • Real home environment
  • Unknown tasks
  • Jury evaluation

Key Technical Challenges

Speech Recognition

Understanding natural language commands in noisy environments with various accents and speaking styles.

Computer Vision

Recognizing objects, people, and gestures in dynamic lighting conditions and cluttered environments.

Manipulation

Grasping and manipulating everyday objects of various shapes, sizes, and materials.

Navigation

Moving safely through dynamic environments with people, furniture, and changing layouts.

Human-Robot Interaction

Natural and intuitive interaction with people of different ages, cultures, and technical backgrounds.

Task Planning

Understanding complex instructions and breaking them down into executable robot actions.

UAE Participation

The UAE is increasingly investing in service robotics research, with several universities and research centers developing capabilities for RoboCup@Home. The focus aligns with the UAE's vision for smart cities and AI-driven services.

  • Khalifa Home Assistants - Khalifa University
  • AUS Smart Home Lab - American University of Sharjah
  • MBZUAI Service Robotics - Mohamed bin Zayed University of AI

Real-World Applications

Elderly & Disability Care

Assistive robots helping with daily tasks, medication reminders, and emergency response for elderly and disabled individuals.

Educational Support

Interactive robots assisting in education, therapy, and skill development for children and adults.
