About RoboCup Soccer

RoboCup Soccer is the flagship competition of RoboCup, featuring autonomous robots playing football in various leagues. The ultimate goal is to develop a team of fully autonomous humanoid robots that can win against the human world champion team in soccer by 2050.

Small Size League (SSL)

Fast-paced games with small robots controlled by a central computer system using overhead cameras.

  • Robot diameter: 180mm max
  • 6 robots per team
  • Global vision system
  • High-speed gameplay

Middle Size League (MSL)

Larger autonomous robots with onboard sensing and computing, playing on a full-size field.

  • Robot diameter: 520mm max
  • 5 robots per team
  • Fully autonomous
  • Onboard vision

Standard Platform League (SPL)

All teams use identical NAO robots, focusing on software development and AI strategies.

  • NAO robots only
  • 7 robots per team
  • Software competition
  • Wireless communication

Humanoid League

Human-like robots playing soccer, divided into different size categories.

  • Kid Size (40-90cm)
  • Adult Size (130-180cm)
  • Bipedal locomotion
  • Human-like appearance

Simulation League

Virtual soccer matches using software agents in a simulated environment.

  • 2D and 3D simulation
  • 11 agents per team
  • AI and strategy focus
  • No hardware required

UAE Participation

The UAE has been actively participating in RoboCup Soccer competitions, with teams from various universities and research institutions. Our teams have shown remarkable progress in recent years, particularly in the Small Size League and Simulation League.

Notable UAE Teams

  • UAEU Falcons - United Arab Emirates University
  • AUS Wolves - American University of Sharjah
  • Khalifa Robotics - Khalifa University
  • Dubai Tech Eagles - Dubai Institute of Technology
