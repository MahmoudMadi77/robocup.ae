RoboCupSoccer

The original and most popular league of RoboCup, featuring autonomous robots playing football

About RoboCupSoccer

RoboCupSoccer is the flagship competition of RoboCup, where teams of autonomous robots compete in football matches. This league serves as a testbed for multi-agent systems, real-time reasoning, and robotics technologies.

Competition Categories

Small Size League (SSL)

Fast-paced games with small robots (≤180mm diameter) on a 12m x 9m field. Features centralized vision and high-speed gameplay.

  • 6 robots per team
  • Global vision system
  • Wireless communication
  • Maximum speed: 3 m/s

Middle Size League (MSL)

Larger autonomous robots (≤52cm diameter) with onboard sensing and processing capabilities.

  • 5 robots per team
  • Onboard vision and processing
  • 18m x 12m field
  • Fully autonomous operation

Standard Platform League (SPL)

Humanoid robots (NAO) competing in football, focusing on software development and AI.

  • 7 NAO robots per team
  • Identical hardware platform
  • Software-only competition
  • Bipedal locomotion

Humanoid League

Various humanoid robots competing in different size classes, from KidSize to AdultSize.

  • KidSize (40-90cm)
  • TeenSize (80-140cm)
  • AdultSize (130-180cm)
  • Custom humanoid platforms

Technical Challenges

Computer Vision

Real-time object detection, tracking, and field localization

Multi-Agent AI

Coordination, strategy, and decision-making in dynamic environments

Motion Control

Precise movement, ball handling, and collision avoidance

Communication

Efficient team communication and information sharing
