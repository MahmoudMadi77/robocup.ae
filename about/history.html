A Brief History of RoboCup UAE

From vision to reality: The journey of robotics competition in the UAE

2017

Foundation

RoboCup UAE was established as the national chapter of the international RoboCup Federation, with the vision of advancing robotics and AI education in the United Arab Emirates.

2018

First National Competition

The inaugural RoboCup UAE competition was held in Dubai, featuring 12 teams from 5 universities across the Emirates. The event focused on RoboCupSoccer and RoboCupJunior leagues.

2019

International Recognition

UAE teams participated in the RoboCup World Championship for the first time, with two teams qualifying from the national competition. This marked UAE's entry into the global RoboCup community.

2020

Digital Transformation

During the global pandemic, RoboCup UAE pioneered virtual competitions and online workshops, maintaining engagement with the robotics community and introducing simulation-based challenges.

2021

League Expansion

RoboCup@Home and RoboCupRescue leagues were introduced, expanding the competition to include service robotics and emergency response scenarios, aligning with UAE's smart city initiatives.

2022

Educational Outreach

Launch of the RoboCup UAE Educational Program, reaching over 100 schools across the Emirates and training more than 500 students in robotics and programming.

2023

Industrial Integration

Introduction of RoboCupIndustrial league, partnering with UAE's manufacturing sector to develop practical robotics solutions for Industry 4.0 applications.

2024

Regional Leadership

RoboCup UAE became the regional hub for Middle East robotics competitions, hosting teams from neighboring countries and establishing the Gulf Robotics Championship.

2025

Future Vision

With over 50 participating teams and partnerships with 15+ universities, RoboCup UAE continues to drive innovation in robotics education and research, supporting UAE Vision 2071.
